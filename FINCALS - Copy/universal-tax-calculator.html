📚 How It Works

Understanding Progressive Taxation

Most countries use progressive tax systems where higher income portions are taxed at higher rates. This calculator applies marginal tax rates correctly—only taxing income within each bracket at that bracket's rate.

📐 The Formula
Tax = Σ (Income in Bracket × Bracket Rate)
Marginal Rate = Tax rate on your next dollar earned
Effective Rate = Total tax ÷ Total income (average rate)
Net Income = Gross income minus total tax liability
Bracket Threshold = Income level where rate changes
Example: With $50,000 income and brackets [10% up to $10k, 20% up to $40k, 30% above], you pay: ($10k×10%) + ($30k×20%) + ($10k×30%) = $1k + $6k + $3k = $10k total tax. Effective rate: 20%, Marginal rate: 30%.

Common questions

Tax brackets use a marginal or progressive system. Only the income falling within each specific bracket is taxed at that rate—not your entire income. For example, if you earn $50,000 and the top bracket is 30% for income over $40,000, only that extra $10,000 is taxed at 30%. Your first $40,000 is taxed at lower rates. This is why your effective tax rate (total tax ÷ income) is always lower than your marginal rate (top bracket rate).
Marginal tax rate is the rate applied to your next dollar of income—essentially your highest tax bracket. Effective tax rate is your actual average rate across all income (total tax ÷ total income). For example, with $100,000 income in the US, your marginal rate might be 24%, but your effective rate could be only 15-18% due to lower brackets on your first dollars earned.
