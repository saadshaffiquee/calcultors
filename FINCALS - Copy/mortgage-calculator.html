📚 How It Works

Understanding Your Mortgage Payment

Your monthly mortgage payment (PITI) includes four components: Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance. Understanding each helps you budget accurately.

📐 The Formula (Principal & Interest)
M = P[r(1+r)^n]/[(1+r)^n-1]
M = Monthly principal & interest payment
P = Loan principal (home price - down payment)
r = Monthly interest rate (annual ÷ 12)
n = Total number of payments (years × 12)
Total Monthly Payment (PITI) = Principal & Interest + Property Taxes/12 + Home Insurance/12 + PMI
🏠 What Makes Up PITI
  • ✓ Principal (P) — The loan amount you actually borrowed
  • ✓ Interest (I) — The cost of borrowing, based on your rate
  • ✓ Taxes (T) — Property taxes paid to your local government
  • ✓ Insurance (I) — Homeowners insurance protecting your property
  • ✓ PMI — Private mortgage insurance (if down payment < 20%)

❓ FAQ

Common questions

Your monthly payment includes PITI: Principal (paying down the loan), Interest (cost of borrowing), Taxes (property tax), and Insurance (homeowners). The principal and interest portion uses this formula: M = P[r(1+r)^n]/[(1+r)^n-1]. Taxes and insurance are annual costs divided by 12. If your down payment is under 20%, you'll also pay PMI.
PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) protects the lender if you default. It's typically required if your down payment is less than 20% of the home price. PMI usually costs 0.3% to 1.5% of the loan amount annually ($75-$375/month on a $300k loan). Once you reach 20% equity through payments or appreciation, you can request PMI removal.
30-year: Lower monthly payments, more flexibility, higher total interest paid. 15-year: Lower interest rate, build equity faster, much less total interest, but higher monthly payments. If you can comfortably afford the 15-year payment, you'll save significantly. However, a 30-year loan with extra principal payments gives you flexibility to reduce payments during hard times.
20% is ideal — avoids PMI, gets better rates, and shows sellers you're serious. However, many lenders accept 3-5% down (FHA loans go as low as 3.5%). With less than 20%, budget for PMI ($100-400/month). First-time buyer programs may offer down payment assistance. Never drain emergency savings for a down payment — keep 3-6 months expenses saved.
For conventional loans: 620+ minimum, but 740+ gets best rates. FHA loans accept 580+ (3.5% down) or 500-579 (10% down). VA loans (veterans) often have no minimum but 620+ is recommended. Each 20-point score increase can save thousands over the loan life. Check your credit 6-12 months before applying to fix errors and improve your score.

On a $320k 30-year loan at 6.5%, paying just $100 extra monthly pays it off 5 years early and saves $87,000 in interest. However, check for prepayment penalties (rare nowadays) and ensure you have emergency savings first. Use our Loan Payoff Calculator to see exact savings.
Interest rate is just the cost of borrowing the principal. APR (Annual Percentage Rate) includes the interest rate PLUS fees (origination, points, mortgage insurance, most closing costs). APR gives you the true cost to compare lenders. If one lender offers 6.5% with $2,000 fees and another offers 6.6% with no fees, the APR helps you see which is actually cheaper.
