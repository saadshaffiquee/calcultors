📚 How It Works

Understanding GPA Calculation

GPA is a weighted average that accounts for both your grades and the credit hours of each course. A 4-credit course affects your GPA twice as much as a 2-credit course.

📐 The Formula
GPA = Σ (Grade Points × Credit Hours) ÷ Σ Credit Hours
Grade Points = Numeric value of your grade (A=4.0, B=3.0, etc.)
Credit Hours = Weight/importance of the course
Quality Points = Grade Points × Credit Hours
CGPA = Total Quality Points ÷ Total Credits (all semesters)

❓ FAQ

Common questions

GPA (Grade Point Average) usually refers to a single semester or term's performance. CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the overall average across all semesters completed. While GPA shows how you performed recently, CGPA reflects your entire academic history. Graduate schools and employers often look at both metrics.
Weighted GPA accounts for course difficulty (honors, AP, IB courses). This calculator uses unweighted 4.0 scale calculations. For weighted GPA, add extra points to your grade points before calculation: typically +0.5 for honors and +1.0 for AP/IB courses. So an A in an AP class would be 5.0 instead of 4.0. Check your school's specific policy as weighting systems vary.
Yes! You can enter grades either as letters (A, B+, C-) or as numeric grade points (4.0, 3.3, 1.7). This is useful if your school uses plus/minus grading with specific decimal values, or if you're calculating based on transcript data that already shows grade points. The calculator accepts any value between 0.0 and 4.0.
This calculator uses the standard 4.0 American scale. If your school uses a different scale (e.g., 5.0, 10.0, or percentage), enter your grades as the equivalent numeric values on the 4.0 scale. For example, if you have 85% and your school considers that a B (3.0), enter "B" or "3.0". Some international systems use 10-point scales where 10 = 4.0, 9 = 3.6, etc.
Pass/fail (or credit/no credit) courses typically do not affect your GPA calculation. If you pass, you earn credit hours but no grade points. If you fail, you earn neither. Do not include pass/fail courses in this calculator unless your school assigns them specific grade points. Always verify with your registrar's policy.
Improving CGPA requires earning higher grades in future courses. Since CGPA is cumulative, each new semester has less impact than the previous ones. To significantly raise your CGPA: (1) Take more credit hours with high grades, (2) Retake failed courses if your school allows grade replacement, (3) Focus on high-credit courses as they have more weight. Use this calculator to simulate different grade scenarios and set realistic targets.
